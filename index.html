Yi Han

Applying Visual Analytics to Large-Scale Data Analysis

Yi is a Research Principle Engineer at Delta Electronics. He received the B.B.A degree in Information Management at National Central Universtiy, Taiwan, and both the M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science at Georgia Institute of Technology. He specializes in the application of visual analytics and has a broad research interest in its intersection with human-computer interaction and ubiquitous computing. The broad research interest can date back to before his Ph.D. studies where he held research positions at VTT Technical Research Centre of Finland, MIT, and Disney Imagineering Research. In his dissertation, advised by Drs. Gregory D. Abowd and John Stasko, he investigated how visual analytics can be applied to analyzing interaction logs of visualization applications. In the future, he is particularly interested in the application of visual analytics in big data analytics where interactive visualizations can not only provide an information-rich front-end user interface but also exploratory analysis capabilities to existing computational analysis platforms.
